## Chapter 1: Opening Day
…

Three days and fifteen seconds ago was when they called an official end to the closure period lasted for ten years or so, and we fail to recognize that some evil had been awaiting all the way till the moment of liberation. Its carnival initiated from the streets, soon extending its grasp to everywhere of the common areas, to beyond the bleak shadows withheld by the grandiose churches where the beloved deity scorns to bless upon, to the dusk of the damp fish market where a blood odor spills over, to the very cutting edges of some bouquets of white calla lilies.

I was left in a hut with the other two. We together lived in a tiny village F. Back in the old days, the village flourished with commuting merchants and ongoing businesses. During the closure, nothing was really going on, so people regress to a life of hunter-gatherer. We retrieved the abandoned skills of agriculture, fishing, and domesticating animals, and after these 10 years of solitude, we became so used to our lives. The open day had actually been appalling to us who haven’t been talking to anyone besides the ones in our community. The exotic expressions of the strangers frightened my eyes, but we did manage to welcome them with the best service since this was the town’s custom.

A rosy beam of shimmer pierced through the shading dawn. And by the far end of the uncanny alley arrived the first guest of the day. The man seemed dazzled by the abundance of architecture in our tiny town. He was chasing aimless after the newborn sun. To the west he ran, where the inscription on signpost marked ‘the fair’. At the time I was setting up my stall selling miscellaneous. An inconsistent figure spurt by. I saw him gasping as mad. Looked like he might be choking anytime.

The man settled down in a moment, had palms put on knees, debilitated. He was at the doorway of pharmacy, the only one in the little town. I heard him exchanging a few words with Daniel the Alchemist who was in charge of all medicine. I watched Dan looking around for something until he fetched a green potion and handed it over to the visitor. Man didn’t hesitate to guzzle. A glass bottle went empty in no time. Looking fulfilled, man left a silver coin and his reflection against the sun. I stared at him for an eternity, and finally his figure disappeared from the horizon. Daniel bowed obsequiously until the man went far, then released his long-held breath that had also been there for ages.

That day hadn’t been any special despite our carnival at night. Obviously, it wasn’t an unusual one. We had just a few pints of bear and some roasted beef. The party was dedicated to the successful opening days. Every member of the community cheered for the tremendous triumph in establishing a connection with the whole world again. These days meant to us the farewell to a much detested and restricted style of life. We were only there to suffer; but now we shall live on and live happily. Daniel was among us the single most active member. Presumably he had only been dealing with his magic items over the years. Nobody believed in magic. It was only him. We sometimes make fun of him. Initially he felt bothered, but the discomfort didn’t last for long. He soon became one of us who would get fanatic with drinks. He was dancing, feasting, devouring, and dancing again. Those made his night. I bet he ran out of energy soon, and that was why me and the party went on when seeing him carried away by his buddies. O he looked sick.

I indulged myself in carnal pleasure through the dim hours, had barely closed my eyes. The second morning I woke up with a cracking head, feeling unconscious of who I was and the situation I was in. The streets were busy and noisy, busy as usual, noisy unusual. The morning was occupied by unpleasant chatters. I didn’t care. I wish I could. I prayed for my dream to continue.

It was mid-day. I woke up again, this time a little more aware of everything. It was a Saturday, peaceful Saturday. Nobody was on the street. Not even the lightest horse hoofs could be perceived. The bell knelled twelve. I realized that my food storage was running out. It was time to get some fresh pork and celery…
