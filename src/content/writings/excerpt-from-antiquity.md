The house is full of dust. As the hidden door gets blown open by a gust of inadvertent wind, those hairballs of different sizes scatter from the floor to the surroundings, floating to the wall, and become embedded in the cracks of the wall. The cracks are encircled with dark bricks and tiles, and occasionally spiders eager to settle down rush to construct their dwellings, thus adding some slight geometrical beauty to this dull building. Of course, there used to be a bright yellow wallpaper as embellishment, but along with the successive alternation of days and nights, the major theme of the room turns light gray.

The house is not ventilated because there is no space for windows; the door is the only proper entrance that the air can ever visit through. There are no such things as air conditions or fans. This is out of tune with the whole world in those extremely feverish seasons.

The lower edges of the three walls in the room are covered by various cardboard boxes, all emptied and discarded, dusted eventually. But they are never neglected. Sparrows nest here. The neatly arranged large boxes reassured the birds. A cluster of multi-generations of bird emerges and lives upon them. In the nest are three broken and one hatching eggs. There is also a new-born baby sparrow feasting on earthworms brought by its mother from afar. So quite often bursts of cheerful chirps would break out from the room.

At the center of the remaining wall is a fireplace connected to the chimney outside. It is made of marble, which now seems to fit very well into the overall impression of the environment. Inside the fireplace was a pile of half-burned wood sticks, but they have already been dyed pitch black. A faint smell of combustion arises when getting close. The ashes left by warming spread unevenly on the floor beyond and look cluttered.

There is a pendulum clock above the fireplace. It appears brand new with little damage to the exterior. The surface of the clock reveals a faint luster, accompanied by an insignificant woody aroma. This valuable is the only existing ornament in the room. Yes, unfortunately, it is a mere decoration now. The hour hand stops at a distant moment and ceases to move forward.

A square table is fitted In the center of the room, together with two chairs, each damaged to varying degrees. There is a green vase in the middle of the table, but the daffodils inside have long since withered and are drooping sullenly. Two more plates are on the table, near the vase, one of them seems to have been emptied, but other carries a piece of moldy bread. The daffodils are withered indeed, but new lives have sprung up on this slice of bread. The flowers replace the bread to dress in a drab brown, the bread, instead, burst into a vibrant green. A stream of ants arrives to join such affair. They crawl on the surface of the bread, devouring along the way. How lively!

Sun sets, leaving the room in endless solitude. In warm glow the day ends and falls the night. The earth gets covered gradually with silvery shiny moonlight. It slips wittingly into the same obsolete space, casting soft shadows on the furniture and lives.
