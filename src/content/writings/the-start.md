It was a midsummer night’s dream. I was staring at the stars from my bed, lost. For not the first time have I questioned the meaning of existence. If emptiness is all that’s left when one passes away, if life is comprised of dreary black and white, why bother to withstand all suffering? Mum entered the stage. She spoke of the story of a man cursed, obligated to roll a rock up to the zenith of a hill; and by the time the rock reaches the top, it inevitably falls back down. I mocked the man who seemed to be enthusiastic about conducting this meaningless act. Years later, when reading Camus’ The Myth of Sisyphus, I realized that as a man who mechanically repeats what has been done previously every other day, I might as well be Sisyphus. But now I am less pessimistic——I see the joy of being a relentless challenger. It is the pleasure that arises from the process of discovering meaning through engaging in what I truly love.

Literature and philosophy redeemed me. A few years ago, as I got lost and questioned about the meaning of existence, I met Don Quixote, a self-claimed knight who saw windmills as giants and fought with them. I mocked the man, crying out that he was a fool for conducting such an absurd act. Mum objected, saying that Don Quixote enjoyed his adventure, for he discovered meaning through it. As for me, she wanted to pass on the idea that life may not seem to present an inherent meaning; we live to obtain it. It is through devoting one’s time to passions that the dreary world becomes revived. For me, that will be done through reading about literature and philosophy.
