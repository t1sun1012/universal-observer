Day 3:

Dear diary, it’s been three days since we moved into the castle in such a remote forest. Everything inside is brand new, and the only flaw is the small crevice on one of the walls. I think it’s connected to a neighboring hut, but I’m not sure.

Day 7:

It was our daughter who firstly noticed the peculiarity of the crevice.

“Mama, something red is shining in the hole.” She said to me.

I came to her, crouched down, and put my face against the wall. There was indeed something red in the hole, emitting maroon beams of light. It was so delicate, just like a ruddy agate. I stared at it for long, out of consciousness. Time seemed no longer to flow… I felt like it became a part of me.

Day 11:

Ever since I first saw that agate, I had become more and more indulged in its beauty. Every afternoon, my husband, daughter and I would squat in front of the hole and just look at the agate. Gradually, I had become more and more abnormal. I would now spend 6-8 hours a day staring at the agate, and the rest of the time it would still fill my mind, even in all my dreams.

Day 17：

I had a whim. I wanted to take that agate out of the hole for a closer view. So, I put my hand through the narrow hole, groping inside, and felt something slimy embedded in a frame. I tried to grab it, but somehow it was always escaping my grope. After a few unsuccessful tries, I brought a knife from kitchen, and reached it into the hole. For some reason, when I took it out, I heard a painful scream. The agate was still beautiful and mesmerizing, but it was engulfed in some kind of fishy and reddish liquid.

Then, I found my husband, daughter and the dog who were also intrigued by the agate. At first, they simply looked at it, but then they started to try to take it away from me. Of course, I wouldn’t let go of my beloved treasure. I was enraged. Why did they want something that belonged to me? I was the one that took it out of the hole, therefore, I was its very rightful owner. Such selfish people! I pointed my knife at them and threatened them to leave, but they have all gone crazy because of the treasure in my hand, as they tried to snatch it from me. Absolutely unacceptable! So, I reached my knife out and sliced their throats, and I was then finally the only one who can enjoy its beauty.

Day 23:

Today, there seemed to be an uninvited visitor who came to the castle. A priest. I was to send him away, but he said that he was here to calm the souls of the dead. I panicked and thought my murder was discovered. So, I invited him into the house as I hid the knife behind my back, ready to slice his throat anytime. But to my surprise, the priest went straight to the crevice.

“I’m very sorry, but” he said. “I feel sorry for the little girl who lived here. She committed suicide recently in her room, by digging one of her eyes out. It was once her only hope. Amen. I still believe that she can go to heaven, as she has always been such a kind kid. Even though she was abused by her father and abandoned at last, she still loves him, perhaps it’s a twisted love. She was constrained in the little room and could only see everything outside through the little hole. She liked to watch her father's every move.”

And he kept on talking.

“I heard her father abandoned her because he was scared, for he always felt a faint chill when his daughter stared at him. He told me that her eyes are as red as precious agates.”

Her eyes are as red as precious agates…
