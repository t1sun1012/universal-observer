I find the pages flipping so quick
On either my notebook of lovely texture
Or on the calendar.
These used to be only days in my world
Now days and nights
Alternate
But blended.
Can’t withhold the artificial lights
From piercing through my undesirable eyes
Even in the darkest moments of night
Restless, relentless
See you on the far end of the journey
Where we do stop the fight
For life.
4.  Rorschach the Man in Mirror
Look in thy glass, and tell the face thou viewest
The glass reflecting a sheer division of black and whiteness
Compositing a world of chaos.
A face
The musk
One thing they are
In glass the Armageddon of the Underworld;
Mask gone
In glass a freak, the stranger to the American Dream
I am not what I am.
5. Sula——A Tiny Tragedy
Chicken little, come and join us!
We got a fun game to play.
Come on, chicken, I’ll help you out.
Come on, down before you break your neck.
‘I ain’t never coming down!’
I’ma tell my brother
If only I have a chance
Before sank into that darkening water
‘Somebody saw.’
6. She who lives in the world of no color
When the world becomes black and grey,
Not anymore
The shelter of newborn prays
Her life is bleak
Her stories break
No remake.
7. The purple romance
I was told that the bats are coming,
From everywhere across the sky, ubiquitous, inescapable.
A plague of bats is coming!
I was told to run
I hesitated
And was tempted by the violet, mysterious, cosmos
That is only to be seen
Here
And
Nowhere else.
Now it is only me and a sky of bats unstitching
Beyond it is an elegant lady
Of prestige and courtesy
Dressing purple.
She is immersed in the purple universe
Playing a purple guitar
The purple fantast companied by a clash of crushing bats
Beauty across the stars.
8. Lysistrata making a shoutout
Women, woman, women!
We have a common task.
We are here for a purpose,
One that is big and meaty
No matter where you’re from
It doesn’t matter.
We are the pioneers of changes.
Keeping the salvation of all Greeks in our hands.
We unite
Till we stop the fights.
9. IB
‘It is the best of the time. It is the worst of the time.’

We are halfway through the journey
A journey across the threshold,
And our real adventure ends up in the skies, in the stars
In the cosmos
In nowhere.
IB sends us to the stadium
We are the gladiators
We combat the math
We defeat the sciences
We are, and will be the winners
We are right on the way heading towards the future.
10. A Theme for English A
English A unlike English B
Scares the students and pushes them away
But for those who stay, well Hurray!
You’ll have in your life a really busy May
That will mean a lot on your way
Way where? Future colored by dull color Grey? Nay!
No need for pray, nor bray
You shall do great
And may the god be there, blessing your play
Saying
Hey!
Your future beautiful as the San Francisco Bay.
11. The Comedian
People judged
He was but a Comedian,
And perhaps a bad one
With his practiced cynicism
Professional jealousy
Cracked
At once.
People commented
No one had been so deliberately amoral.
He was born for madness
The pointless butchery.
But he was different
He understood perfectly.
Those, people thought
And pretended not to care
It was a joke!
It’s all a joke!
A gag
It stinks.
No!
Forgive me
What’s so funny?
…
The comedian knew
In front of him
Was the American dream
He was looking at it
Along with women, children, specters
Blasted into nothing.
The comedian saw through
The cracks in society
The little man in masks
The filth of the century
He embraced them and became further a parody
A reflection of the evil.
Once, the Comedian told a joke
Man goes to doctor
Depressed
Says life seems harsh and cruel
Answered
Simple treatment
Find Clown Pagliacci
He shall pick you up
But Doctor
I am Pagliacci
What’s so funny?
…
It’s time.
Roll on snare drum.
Curtains.
12. Dr. Manhattan——Being a God
It is the first day when I start to fix the clock.
It is my last day being human.
It is my first glance at her.
It is my last compassion to them.
Time is linear.
Everything is happening to me.
I understand that everything,
But not you, girl
You miracle of thermodynamics
I never see through your abyss-like pupils
Like I do to the atoms.
I am glad to meet you.
I am afraid to lose you.
I lost you.
Time for a new journey.
Not being a God!
Just time to create some extra Homo Sapiens.
13. Peace
What is peace?
Philosophers advocate peace.
Politicians favor peace.
People demand peace.
But what if I tell you,
Weapons do guarantee that peace?
Yet,
That Peace is a pulp
That produces mass predicament.
That promotes death rate proliferation.
Some purging psychic attacks.
A preacher for an unpromising
Non-persistent pursue of peace.
Do KNOW, that it comes down to a prank!
Period.
14. 20 Years of Solitude
Speaking of my journeys
Neither epic nor cheery
Of the many pains I suffered,
My heart stuck on the open sea.
I missed only the land and my family
Her
My gorgeous lady
Penelope of great wary.
A trip with nymph
But my heart surrenders not to seductions.
You are my one and only
Despite my second and third sonny.
Anyway, I shall be heading home
With that nostalgia in mind
Always pleasure to envision the return.
Not now.
O!
I solemnly question
How many more year should your curse last?
Poseidon!
Not of the piggy tail phantom
But one that binds me to stay with her
Calypso, right next to me.
15. Beyond the Odyssey
What people know of the Odyssey:
Man out for a war.
Man wanders for years.
Man thinks of his home.
Man gets back home.
What people will never know of the Odyssey:
Women are there, as frequently as the man.
Man thinks of his home yet stays on strange island for years.
Man being disloyal.
Man achieving everything with the assistance of the gods.
Man gets back home.
The bloody stories behind the scenes.
People overlook
These seem to go beyond The Odyssey.
16. Sula——Speaking of Justice
The story begins in the Bottom
Bottom of the heaven
Where land is fertile
Crops grow for a mile
People live heavenly.
The bottom is on top of the hill
Where residents look down
Overlooking other communities.
Residents are the laborious birds
Their lords offer them the best piece of land
Saying that they will do a little sacrifice.
The lords are being gazed upon
Their land sterile and people in hunger
The lords are the caged bird
Justice is achieved
A world of no exploitations
And no inequality!
That’s the story being told.
That’s when the lords speak of justice.
17. Man from the Underground
Hatred, rooted and buried
Now everything in his mind.
On the street, those who praised
Turns their head
On their face leaks out cunning laughter
Bitter sour.
Switch back exhibit a grim expression
Appears as the new fashion.
It’s when the doomsday clock knells for the latest Armageddon.
He hears it at random.
And him, the only
Amongst the phantom.
Clock stroke the twelfth times
Mind blowing.
Only in anger he finds out about the unusual matters,
That street people wear masks with them
Under the hood some appalling monsters
Filthy, evil
And hopelessly
Gray.
He is himself black and white.
The volatile color dots dance in chaos.
But they will never blend,
Not even in the face of Armageddon.
Never compromise!
When the black freighter threatens to crush the street,
In all the dreams before that helpless sight,
Those men of sin look up and shout
SAVE US!
And he shall look down,
Whisper
NO.
The evil has to be punished
At the cost of some fragile peace
No compromise!
Was named Walter Joseph Kovacs.
Known as Rorschach.
On a street of love,
On a street of lonely, austere offices,
Wherever,
The end is nigh.

Rebellious knight Modred, came to combat using sword, shield either.

Never known by “naïve” Modred, he was fighting his father.
Blood fog and murderous spirit enveloped the originally splendid city.
Whines could be heard everywhere, knights were screaming and shouting.
All these were caused by Modred’s loot of power and beauty.
Eventually both of their legendary life came to an ending.
