“Who’s there?” once again, the woman screamed in terror. Her green eyes was full of fright, her voice trembled as the knife in her hand shook uncontrollably.

“Food…” the mysterious figure murmured as it appeared behind the table, “I am here… to search for food!”

The strange figure leaped towards her, its bloody mouth wide open…

Tasi’s eyes opened wide as she sat up on her bed in indescribable horror. The nightmare had been bothering her as soon as she moved here. It seemed like coming to this solitary hut in the middle of the woods wasn’t a good idea whatsoever. As a female novelist, her singularity has saved her from the wicked manipulation of Victorian men, but has created unexpected disdain and purposeful social isolation. Due to the unfriendliness of the world towards Tasi, she decided to move here to calm down, and to also look for inspirations.

She has nothing around her now except for all the stories that were left in her mind. She was indulged in all sort of scenario that happens in her story. She’d even input herself into the story, to seek for that sense of solitary, and fancy life. Unfortunately, before she could even truly enjoy the magic of the woods, the figure in her dreams began to stalk and trouble her even more mentally. She called the figure Boogeyman, also one of her story character. Every night, the Boogeyman creeped into a hut. Every night, the Boogeyman got closer to its target. And every night, the poor victim asks the same question: “Who’s there?”,but the Boogeyman never responded. That was its setting in her story, she felt satisfied about the monster, as an author of horror story.

Until the very night when the victim became Tasi herself, truly in reality.

Tasi was roaming aimlessly in the woods. The sun was getting low. Her heart felt heavier, almost if it is trying to escape her chest. She felt that she is being watched

She is being watched.

Eventually, the sun set down. The full moon replaced it, its light engulfed the woods. Tasi didn’t want to sleep. She knows that the boogeyman was waiting for her in another nightmare, in search of food. She thought as long as she stayed awake, she would be fine.

The clock ticked ten. Tasi was wide awake. Silence.

The clock ticked eleven. Tasi was wide awake. Silence.

The clock ticked twelve. Tasi was still wide awake.

“Pam!” the silence was broken by a bright clash from the kitchen.

Tasi rose. She walked to the kitchen. On the floor was a broken cup. She turned around. Then she saw a shadow sliding across the floor.

In panic, Tasi picked up a knife from a drawer. As she turned around again, she saw the mysterious figure that haunted her in the dreams for the last few nights.

“Who’s there?” once again, Tasi screamed in terror. Her green eyes was full of fright, her voice trembled as the knife in her hand shook uncontrollably.

The figure appeared. It was a naked man. Actually, it was half a naked man. It seemed like he was cut into 2 identical halves, and this is one of the pieces. He had one arm, one leg, and one eye. His skin was crumbled and rotten. His only eye was white and had white disgusting sores. Most of his teeth fell off, and the rest are sharp like spearheads.

“Food…” the mysterious figure murmured as it appeared behind the table, “I am here… to search for food!”

The strange figure leaped towards her, its bloody mouth wide open…

It was the end of the story for Tasi, but not for the monster. Just in another hut in a remote forest, coincidentally, the owner of it was also troubled by the same nightmare. However, instead of the naked man in Tais’s dream, the figure in his dream was a naked woman with one green eye.

The Boogeymen.
